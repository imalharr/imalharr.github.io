I'm Megh. I'm 15, from Howrah, and I've never fit cleanly into one category — which stopped being a problem the moment I stopped trying to.

I build hardware because I like making things that physically exist and do something real. I chair MUN committees because I like systems — how rules get made, how arguments are structured, how governance actually works under pressure. I write because I have things to say and I care about saying them well. These aren't separate hobbies. They're the same instinct pointing in different directions.

I just finished Class 10 CBSE boards and I'm moving into Class 11 PCM + Computer Science. I want to build at the intersection of physical and digital — embedded systems, IoT, whatever sits at that edge. Long term, no fixed answer yet. But I know what kind of work I want to do: the kind that solves something real.
