cat how-i-work.txt

Editing is about deciding what to cut, not what to include. The instinct for pacing — knowing when a shot has overstayed, when a cut should come earlier — is mostly built by watching a lot and then doing a lot. I've been doing both.

Color grading is the part I care most about. It sets the tone more than most people realize. The trek footage had wildly inconsistent lighting — bright beach sun, dim evening, cloudy midday. Getting it to feel like one cohesive thing took more time than the actual assembly edit.

Sound design is usually the most underestimated part. In the Annual Day productions, getting the SFX to land at exactly the right frame made the difference between something that felt polished and something that just felt edited.
