Case Study

NAS Coastal Trek Documentary (March 2026)

During the 44km coastal conservation trek through Odisha, I captured footage of wildlife, team moments, and the Olive Ridley turtle rescue. The challenge was editing raw, unstructured footage into a cohesive narrative that conveyed both the adventure and the conservation message.

Editing Approach

Narrative Arc: Structured footage to follow the journey: preparation, arrival, discovery, rescue, reflection.
Emotional Pacing: Used music and cuts to build tension during the turtle rescue, then resolve with reflection.
Visual Consistency: Color-graded all footage to match, despite varying lighting conditions and camera equipment.
Sound Design: Layered ambient sounds, music, and dialogue to create immersion without overwhelming the viewer.
Message Clarity: Ensured the conservation message was clear without being preachy.

Impact

The documentary was featured in the Aajkal newspaper and the Kolkata Rotary Club's "Shine" magazine. It helped raise awareness about coastal conservation and Olive Ridley turtles. The video became a resource for the school's environmental club.

Process

How I Work

Understand the Vision: Before touching any footage, I meet with stakeholders to understand the goal, tone, and target audience.
Organize Assets: Sort and label all footage, audio, and graphics. A clean project structure saves hours later.
Rough Cut: Create a first pass focusing on pacing and narrative flow, ignoring polish.
Sound Design: Add music, sound effects, and dialogue. Often the most impactful part of editing.
Color Grading: Create visual consistency and set the mood through color correction.
VFX & Graphics: Add titles, transitions, and visual effects. These should enhance, not distract.
Review & Iterate: Get feedback from stakeholders and refine based on their input.
Final Export: Render in appropriate formats for different platforms (4K for archival, 1080p for web, etc.).
