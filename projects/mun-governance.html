Codex Magnifica
Comprehensive chair's guide for PMUN 2025-26. Committee rules, judging criteria, crisis management protocol, delegate support guidelines. Written from scratch — no template.
Evaluation Rubric
Multi-dimensional rubric assessing research, diplomacy, public speaking, crisis response, and committee participation. Designed so two judges scoring the same delegate get similar results.

Transparent point allocation for speeches, resolutions, and crisis responses. Verifiable. No hidden criteria.

Public Speaking
20 pts

Crisis Response
20 pts
Handling unexpected situations. In-character thinking. Creative problem solving.
Participation
15 pts
Consistent engagement. Meaningful contribution throughout, not just big moments.
Why this works
100 pts

cat chair-notes.txt

Hardest part
Deciding who wins. The rubric helps, but when two delegates have both done excellent work in completely different ways — one a sharp negotiator, one a meticulous researcher — the final call still comes down to judgment. No system eliminates that.
What I learned
Delegates need to trust the judging process completely or the whole simulation breaks. Transparency in criteria matters more than getting every score exactly right.
